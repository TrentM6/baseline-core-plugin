## Sales Deck Structure

### The Narrative Arc

**Situation → Problem → Solution → Proof → Ask**

**1. Situation (1-2 slides):**
Set the scene. What's happening in the prospect's world?
- Industry trend or market shift
- Common challenge their segment faces
- Frame it as their world, not yours

**2. Problem (2-3 slides):**
Sharpen the pain. Make them feel it.
- Specific consequences of the status quo
- What it costs them (time, money, risk, competitive position)
- Why existing solutions fall short

**3. Solution (3-5 slides):**
Your product as the answer to their problem.
- Lead with the outcome, not the feature
- Show how it works (demo, screenshots, workflow)
- Map capabilities to their specific pain points
- Keep technical details proportional to the audience

**4. Proof (2-3 slides):**
Evidence that it works.
- Customer stories relevant to their segment
- Metrics and outcomes (specific numbers, not vague claims)
- Logos, testimonials, case studies
- Third-party validation (analyst reports, awards, press)

**5. Ask (1 slide):**
Clear next step.
- Not "buy now" — match the next step to the deal stage
- Early: "Let's run a pilot" or "Let's do a deeper discovery"
- Mid: "Here's a proposal for your review"
- Late: "Let's align on timeline and get this started"

### Deck Rules

- Under 15 slides for a first meeting
- More visuals, fewer walls of text
- One point per slide
- Every slide should earn its place — if removing it doesn't hurt, remove it
- Customize 2-3 slides per prospect (their logo, their use case, their metrics)

## Discovery Call Frameworks

### BANT (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline)

**Best for:** Qualifying early-stage leads quickly.

| Question Area | Sample Questions |
|--------------|-----------------|
| **Budget** | "Is there a budget allocated for this initiative?" / "What's the investment range you're considering?" |
| **Authority** | "Who else is involved in this decision?" / "What does your evaluation process look like?" |
| **Need** | "What's driving this initiative?" / "What happens if you don't solve this?" |
| **Timeline** | "When do you need this in place?" / "Is there an event driving the timeline?" |

**Limitation:** Focuses on qualification, not understanding. Use for initial screening, not deep discovery.

### MEDDIC (Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion)

**Best for:** Enterprise and complex sales with long cycles.

| Element | What to Discover | Why It Matters |
|---------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Metrics** | How they'll measure success | Anchors the value conversation |
| **Economic Buyer** | Who signs the check | Don't sell to someone who can't buy |
| **Decision Criteria** | What factors drive their choice | Lets you position against criteria |
| **Decision Process** | Steps, timeline, stakeholders | Predicts deal velocity and risks |
| **Identify Pain** | The specific problem (quantified) | Pain drives urgency |
| **Champion** | Your internal advocate | Someone who sells when you're not in the room |

### SPIN (Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-Payoff)

**Best for:** Consultative selling where you need to uncover latent needs.

| Phase | Purpose | Example Questions |
|-------|---------|------------------|
| **Situation** | Understand current state | "Walk me through how your team currently handles [process]" |
| **Problem** | Surface explicit problems | "What's the biggest challenge with that approach?" |
| **Implication** | Expand the problem's impact | "When that happens, how does it affect [downstream outcome]?" |
| **Need-Payoff** | Get them to articulate the value of solving it | "If you could [outcome], what would that mean for your team?" |

### When to Use Which

| Framework | Deal Size | Complexity | Best When |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| BANT | Any | Low-Medium | Qualifying volume leads quickly |
| MEDDIC | $50K+ | High | Enterprise, long sales cycles |
| SPIN | Any | Medium-High | Consultative, relationship-driven |

## Objection Handling

### The Framework: Acknowledge → Clarify → Respond → Confirm

**1. Acknowledge:** Show you heard them. Don't dismiss or immediately counter.
- "That's a fair concern."
- "I hear you — pricing is always an important consideration."

**2. Clarify:** Make sure you understand the real objection.
- "Can you tell me more about what's behind that?"
- "When you say it's too expensive, compared to what?"

**3. Respond:** Address the objection directly with evidence.
- Data, case studies, testimonials, ROI calculation
- Reframe if the objection is based on a misunderstanding

**4. Confirm:** Check that you've addressed it.
- "Does that address your concern?"
- "How does that land with you?"

### Common Objections and Responses

| Objection | Likely Root Cause | Response Direction |
|-----------|------------------|-------------------|
| "It's too expensive" | Price vs. value gap | Quantify ROI, compare to cost of status quo |
| "We're happy with current solution" | Inertia, risk aversion | Surface hidden pain, show switching cost vs. staying cost |
| "We don't have time to implement" | Fear of disruption | Show phased implementation, quick wins, support plan |
| "We need to think about it" | Unclear value or unstated concern | Ask what they need to decide, offer to address specific questions |
| "Can you add [feature]?" | Possible dealbreaker or negotiation tactic | Understand must-have vs. nice-to-have, share roadmap if relevant |
| "My boss won't approve this" | Champion needs ammo | Provide business case materials, offer to present to the decision-maker |

## Demo Best Practices

### Structure

**1. Set context (2 min):**
Recap their situation and pain from discovery. Confirm it's still accurate.

**2. Show value early (5 min):**
Start with the most impactful thing — not the login screen. Show the outcome they care about first.

**3. Walk their use case (10-15 min):**
Demonstrate using their scenario, their terminology, their data (if possible). This is their story, not a generic walkthrough.

**4. Handle questions in real-time:**
Pause for questions at natural break points. Don't save them all for the end.

**5. Close with next steps (3 min):**
Summarize value shown. Propose the next step.

### Demo Rules

- **Customize:** Use their logo, their terms, their use case. Generic demos feel generic.
- **Rehearse:** Run through the demo at least once before showing it live. Check for data issues, loading times, edge cases.
- **Tell, don't just show:** Narrate what you're doing and why it matters. "Notice how this saves the step where you'd normally have to..."
- **Handle interruptions gracefully:** If they ask about something you're about to show, pivot to it now.
- **Skip the boring parts:** Nobody needs to watch you fill in a form. Pre-fill setup steps.
- **Have a backup plan:** Screenshots or recording ready in case the live product has issues.

## Proposal/SOW Guidance

### Proposal Structure

1. **Executive summary** — Why this, why now, why you (half page)
2. **Understanding of needs** — Restate their situation and goals (shows you listened)
3. **Proposed solution** — What you'll deliver, how it works
4. **Scope of work** — Specific deliverables, timelines, milestones
5. **Investment** — Pricing (present clearly, not buried)
6. **Timeline** — Key dates, dependencies
7. **Team** — Who they'll work with (bios if relevant)
8. **Terms** — Payment, cancellation, SLA
9. **Next steps** — How to move forward

### Pricing Presentation

**Anchoring:** Present the most comprehensive option first, then the recommended option.

**Options:** Offer 2-3 tiers when possible:
- **Good:** Core solution, covers their stated needs
- **Better:** Recommended — core + strategic additions
- **Best:** Premium — everything, for clients who want maximum value

**Avoid:**
- Hiding pricing until asked (builds distrust)
- Single take-it-or-leave-it pricing (no flexibility feels rigid)
- Line-item pricing for every component (invites cherry-picking)

## Sales-Marketing Alignment

### Lead Handoff

**Marketing Qualified Lead (MQL) → Sales Qualified Lead (SQL):**

Define clear criteria for when a lead moves from marketing to sales:

| MQL Criteria (Marketing) | SQL Criteria (Sales) |
|--------------------------|---------------------|
| Downloaded key content | Budget confirmed |
| Attended webinar/event | Decision-maker identified |
| Visited pricing page 2+ times | Timeline within 6 months |
| Matches ICP firmographics | Specific need articulated |
| Requested demo | Champion identified |

**Handoff process:**
1. Marketing notifies sales with lead context (source, content consumed, engagement history)
2. Sales follows up within 24 hours (48 max)
3. Sales updates CRM with qualification outcome
4. If disqualified, sales records why → marketing adjusts targeting

### Feedback Loops

| From Sales to Marketing | From Marketing to Sales |
|------------------------|----------------------|
| "Prospects are asking about [topic]" → Content creation | "This blog post drove 50 MQLs" → Sales follow-up |
| "This objection keeps coming up" → Collateral update | "Webinar attendees have 2x close rate" → Prioritize webinar leads |
| "Competitors mentioned in deals" → Competitive content | "New case study available" → Deal support material |

### Shared Metrics

Both teams should own:
- Pipeline generated (new opportunities created)
- Pipeline velocity (time from MQL to close)
- Win rate by lead source
- Revenue from marketing-sourced leads

## Collateral by Deal Stage

### Awareness Stage

**Goal:** Get on their radar. Build trust and credibility.

| Collateral | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| Blog posts / articles | Thought leadership, SEO |
| Social media content | Brand awareness |
| Industry reports | Credibility |
| Webinars / events | Engagement |

### Consideration Stage

**Goal:** Help them evaluate. Show you understand their problem.

| Collateral | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| Case studies | Proof it works for companies like theirs |
| Product overview / demo video | Show the solution |
| Comparison guides | Position against alternatives |
| ROI calculator | Quantify the value |
| Customer testimonials | Social proof |

### Decision Stage

**Goal:** Remove risk. Make buying easy.

| Collateral | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| Detailed proposal / SOW | Scope and terms |
| Implementation plan | Show the path forward |
| Security/compliance docs | Remove blockers |
| Reference calls | Peer validation |
| Pricing options | Clear investment |
| Contract / order form | Frictionless close |
