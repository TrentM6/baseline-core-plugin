# Go-to-Market Planning

Go-to-market planning is the discipline of bringing products to users in a way that drives adoption and revenue. It connects product, marketing, and sales strategy into a coherent plan for reaching and converting the right customers.

## Core Principles

1. **Product-market fit first.** GTM can't fix a product people don't want.
2. **One channel deeply before spreading wide.** Master one acquisition channel before adding more.
3. **Price reflects value, not cost.** Pricing is a strategic decision, not a math problem.
4. **Free to paid is a product problem.** If users don't convert, the product isn't delivering enough value.
5. **Distribution is the strategy.** How you reach users matters as much as what you build.
6. **Simple pricing, clear value.** If users can't understand your pricing in 10 seconds, simplify.

## Workflow

### 1. Clarify Before Starting
- What's the product/feature being launched?
- Who is the target audience?
- What's the pricing model?
- What channels are available?
- What's the competitive landscape?
- What does success look like?

### 2. Choose the Right Approach

| Need | Approach |
|------|----------|
| New product launch | Full GTM plan |
| Feature launch | Launch brief + channel strategy |
| Pricing decision | Pricing analysis + competitive context |
| Channel expansion | Channel strategy + experimentation |
| Adoption problems | Conversion analysis + intervention |

### 3. GTM Process

**Strategy:** Define ICP > set pricing > choose channels > plan launch sequence.

**Prepare:** Create messaging > enable sales > prepare support > instrument analytics.

**Launch:** Execute channel plan > monitor metrics > gather feedback > iterate.

**Optimize:** Analyze conversion > optimize channels > refine pricing > scale what works.

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Instead |
|--------------|---------|
| Launch without ICP | Define who it's for first |
| Price based on cost | Price based on value |
| All channels at once | Master one, then expand |
| Launch and forget | Plan post-launch optimization |
| No sales enablement | Equip everyone who touches customers |
