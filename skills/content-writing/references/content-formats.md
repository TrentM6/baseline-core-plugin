# Content Formats

Format-specific guides for each content type. Load this when drafting content to follow best practices for the chosen format.

---

## Blog Post / Article

**Structure:** Hook → Main argument → Supporting points → Conclusion + CTA

**Hook patterns that work:**
- Start with a contrarian take: "Most companies get [topic] wrong. Here's why."
- Start with a specific result: "We reduced churn by 40% by changing one thing."
- Start with a question the reader is already asking: "Why does [common frustration] keep happening?"
- Start with a story: One paragraph, specific, relatable.

**Writing rules:**
- One clear takeaway per post. State it in the first 2 paragraphs.
- Subheadings every 2-3 paragraphs — readers scan before they read.
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max). White space is your friend.
- Use concrete examples, not abstract claims.
- End with a specific CTA, not "let us know what you think."

**Length guide:**
- Quick insight: 500-800 words
- Standard post: 800-1500 words
- Deep dive: 1500-2500 words
- Pillar content: 2500-4000 words

## Newsletter

**Structure:** Opening hook → 1-3 main items → CTA or takeaway

**Types:**
- **Curated:** 3-5 links with your commentary on why they matter
- **Single-topic:** One idea explored in depth (most effective for thought leadership)
- **Update:** What happened, what's next, why it matters to the reader
- **Hybrid:** One main piece + a few quick links or updates

**Writing rules:**
- Subject line: specific and curiosity-driven, not clickbait
- First line earns the open. Don't waste it on "Happy Tuesday!"
- Write like you're emailing one person, not a list
- Keep it scannable — bold key phrases, use short paragraphs
- One CTA per newsletter. More than one = none.

**Length:** 300-800 words (readers skim newsletters — respect that)

## LinkedIn Post

**Structure:** Hook line → Insight or story → Takeaway → CTA (optional)

**Hook patterns:**
- Bold statement: "Stop doing X. It's costing you Y."
- Personal story opener: "Last week I [specific thing]..."
- Data point: "87% of [audience] struggle with [problem]."
- Question: "What's the one thing you'd change about [topic]?"

**Writing rules:**
- First line is everything — it's the only thing visible before "see more"
- One idea per post. Not three tips. One idea.
- Short lines. Break after every sentence or two.
- Use "I" and "we" — LinkedIn rewards personal voice
- End with a question or clear takeaway, not a hashtag dump
- 3-5 hashtags max, placed at the end

**Length:** 100-300 words (sweet spot is 150-200)

**Formatting:**
- Line breaks between every 1-2 sentences
- No walls of text
- Bold or caps sparingly for emphasis
- Lists work, but keep them short (3-5 items)

## Twitter/X Thread

**Structure:** Hook tweet → Supporting tweets → Summary/CTA tweet

**Writing rules:**
- Tweet 1 must stand alone and make people want to read more
- Each tweet should make sense on its own AND connect to the thread
- Number your tweets (1/10) or use a clear flow
- One idea per tweet
- End the thread with a summary + CTA (follow, link, reply)
- Pin the thread or quote-tweet it for visibility

**Length:** 5-15 tweets (sweet spot is 7-10)

## Email Campaign

**Types:**
- **Welcome sequence:** Introduce yourself, set expectations, deliver value early
- **Nurture sequence:** Build trust over time, share insights, soft CTAs
- **Launch sequence:** Build anticipation, reveal, offer, deadline
- **Re-engagement:** "We miss you" with a compelling reason to come back

**Structure per email:** Subject → First line (why you're writing) → Body → CTA

**Writing rules:**
- Subject line: specific, curiosity-driven, under 50 characters
- First line: get to the point — why should they read this?
- One CTA per email. Be specific: "Reply with your biggest challenge" not "Click here"
- Write short. Under 300 words per email in most sequences.
- Preview text matters — write it intentionally, don't let it auto-generate

**Sequence pacing:**
- Welcome: 3-5 emails over 7-14 days
- Nurture: weekly or biweekly
- Launch: 3-7 emails over 5-10 days
- Re-engagement: 2-3 emails over 7 days, then stop

## Case Study Write-Up

**Structure:** Context → Challenge → Solution → Results → Takeaway

**Writing rules:**
- Lead with the result: "Company X reduced onboarding time by 60%"
- Be specific about the challenge — what was actually broken?
- Show the solution in action, not just describe it
- Use real numbers. Percentages, timelines, dollar amounts.
- Include a direct quote from the customer if possible
- End with a takeaway that's relevant to the reader, not just the customer

**Length:** 500-1500 words

**Formats:**
- Long-form (blog-style narrative): 1000-1500 words
- Short-form (structured sections): 500-800 words
- One-pager (for sales): 300-500 words with key metrics highlighted

## Thought Leadership

**What it is:** Content that advances a perspective, not just reports information. You're arguing for a point of view.

**Structure:** Thesis → Evidence → Implications → What to do about it

**Writing rules:**
- Have a real opinion. "It depends" is not thought leadership.
- Back your opinion with evidence — data, experience, case studies
- Acknowledge the counterargument, then explain why you disagree
- Make it actionable — don't just diagnose, prescribe
- Write from experience, not from research alone

**Length:** 1500-3000 words

## Social Media Content (General)

**Platform-agnostic rules:**
- Native content outperforms links. Write for the platform, don't just drop a URL.
- Visual content gets more engagement. Pair with images, screenshots, or video when possible.
- Engage in comments. Posting without responding is broadcasting, not social media.
- Repurpose, don't repeat. Same idea, different angle for each platform.
- Post consistently. Sporadic posting kills momentum.
