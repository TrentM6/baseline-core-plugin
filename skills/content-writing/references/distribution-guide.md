# Distribution Guide

Platform-specific rules, best practices, and content adaptation strategies. Load this when publishing or repurposing content across platforms.

---

## Platform Quick Reference

| Platform | Best for | Tone | Length | Frequency |
|----------|----------|------|--------|-----------|
| Blog/Website | SEO, depth, thought leadership | Varies (follow voice.md) | 800-2500 words | 1-4x/month |
| LinkedIn | Professional audience, B2B, thought leadership | Professional but personal | 100-300 words | 3-5x/week |
| Twitter/X | Quick insights, engagement, real-time | Concise, punchy | 280 chars / 5-15 tweet threads | Daily |
| Newsletter | Nurturing existing audience | Conversational, personal | 300-800 words | Weekly or biweekly |
| Email campaign | Direct conversion, onboarding, launches | Direct, value-focused | 150-400 words/email | Per sequence |
| YouTube | Tutorials, demos, thought leadership | Conversational, clear | 5-15 min (short) / 15-45 min (long) | 1-4x/month |
| Instagram | Visual storytelling, culture, behind-the-scenes | Casual, visual-first | Caption: 50-150 words | 3-5x/week |

## Repurposing Strategy

One piece of content should become many. Start with your highest-effort format and break it down.

**The cascade:**
1. **Long-form content** (blog post, thought leadership article)
2. → **LinkedIn post** (pull one key insight, rewrite for LinkedIn format)
3. → **Twitter/X thread** (break the argument into tweet-sized pieces)
4. → **Newsletter** (summarize + add personal commentary)
5. → **Social clips** (pull 1-2 quotable lines for quick posts)
6. → **Email** (use the insight as the hook for a nurture email)

**Rules for repurposing:**
- Don't copy-paste. Rewrite for each platform's native format.
- Each version should stand alone — don't assume the reader saw the original.
- Change the hook. What works on a blog doesn't work on LinkedIn.
- Stagger timing. Don't post everywhere on the same day.

## Blog / Website

**SEO basics:**
- Write for humans first, search engines second
- Use one primary keyword naturally in the title, first paragraph, and 2-3 subheadings
- Meta description: 150-160 characters, include the keyword, make it compelling
- Internal links to related content (2-3 per post)
- External links to credible sources where relevant

**Publishing checklist:**
- [ ] Title is clear and includes the primary keyword
- [ ] Meta description written (not auto-generated)
- [ ] Featured image selected
- [ ] Internal links added
- [ ] CTA is clear and specific
- [ ] Proofread for voice.md compliance

## LinkedIn

**Algorithm notes:**
- Text-only posts and carousels tend to outperform link posts
- Early engagement (first 60 minutes) matters most
- Comments are weighted more than reactions
- Posts with "dwell time" (people stop scrolling to read) get boosted

**Best practices:**
- Post during business hours (Tue-Thu tend to perform best)
- Reply to every comment in the first hour
- Tag people only when genuinely relevant
- Use carousels (PDF uploads) for multi-point content
- Don't edit posts within the first hour (can hurt reach)

**What to post:**
- Lessons learned from real experience
- Behind-the-scenes of your work or decisions
- Takes on industry trends (with your perspective)
- Customer wins and case studies (with permission)
- Questions that invite genuine discussion

**What to avoid:**
- "I'm humbled to announce..." posts
- Engagement bait ("Like if you agree!")
- Posting links in the main post (put them in comments if needed)
- Generic motivational content

## Twitter/X

**Algorithm notes:**
- Threads get more reach than single tweets for long-form
- Quote tweets with commentary outperform plain retweets
- Images and video increase engagement
- Replies and conversations boost visibility

**Best practices:**
- Tweet consistently (daily if possible)
- Engage with others before and after posting
- Use threads for anything that needs more than 2 sentences
- Pin your best-performing or most relevant tweet
- Schedule tweets during peak hours for your audience

## Newsletter (Email)

**Deliverability basics:**
- Consistent send schedule builds trust with email providers
- Clean your list regularly (remove bounces and long-term non-openers)
- Avoid spam trigger words in subject lines
- Plain text or simple HTML outperforms heavy design
- Include an unsubscribe link (required by law)

**Subject line formulas:**
- Specific + curiosity: "The onboarding mistake that costs 23% of new users"
- Direct value: "How to [achieve result] in [timeframe]"
- Personal: "I was wrong about [topic]"
- Question: "Are you making this [topic] mistake?"

**Growth tactics:**
- CTA to subscribe on every blog post
- Welcome sequence that delivers immediate value
- Cross-promote on social (share a preview, link to subscribe)
- Make sharing easy (include a "forward to a friend" CTA)

## Content Calendar Principles

**Don't plan content more than 4-6 weeks out.** Topics go stale. Leave room for timely, reactive content.

**Balance your content mix:**
- 60% value content (teaches, informs, helps)
- 20% thought leadership (your perspective, opinions, takes)
- 10% social proof (case studies, testimonials, wins)
- 10% promotional (product, offers, CTAs)

**Batching:**
- Write in batches (2-3 pieces at once) for consistency
- Schedule distribution, but post reactively when timely
- Review performance monthly — double down on what works

## Measuring What Matters

| Content Type | Primary Metric | Secondary Metric |
|-------------|---------------|-----------------|
| Blog post | Organic traffic, time on page | Backlinks, email signups |
| LinkedIn post | Impressions, comments | Profile views, connection requests |
| Newsletter | Open rate, click rate | Reply rate, unsubscribe rate |
| Email campaign | Click rate, conversion | Open rate, reply rate |
| Twitter/X | Impressions, replies | Follows, link clicks |
| Case study | Page views, time on page | Influenced pipeline |

**What to track:**
- Which topics get the most engagement?
- Which formats perform best on each platform?
- Where do readers drop off?
- What's driving signups, demos, or conversions?

**What to ignore:**
- Vanity metrics without context (follower count alone)
- Comparing across platforms (different audiences, different metrics)
- Short-term dips (content compounds over months, not days)
