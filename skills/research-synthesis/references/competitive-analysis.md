## Competitive Landscape Mapping

### Competitor Types

**Direct competitors:** Same solution, same audience.
- They do what you do for who you serve
- Example: Figma vs. Sketch (both design tools for product teams)

**Indirect competitors:** Different solution, same problem.
- They solve the same user problem a different way
- Example: Figma vs. pen-and-paper prototyping

**Substitute competitors:** Different category, competing for the same budget or attention.
- They don't seem like competitors but absorb the same resources
- Example: An in-house design team vs. a design tool subscription

### Mapping Exercise

**Step 1: Brainstorm all competitors** (direct, indirect, substitute). Aim for 15-30 names.

**Step 2: Classify and prioritize.** Most teams should deeply track 3-5 direct competitors and loosely monitor 5-10 indirect/substitutes.

**Step 3: Create a positioning map.**
Choose two axes that matter to your users (e.g., price vs. capability, ease-of-use vs. power).

```
High Capability
      |
  [Competitor A]     [Competitor B]
      |
      |         [YOU]
      |
  [Competitor C]     [Competitor D]
      |
Low Capability
      |
Low Price ————————————————— High Price
```

The goal isn't precision — it's to visualize where you sit relative to alternatives.

## Competitive Intelligence Sources

### Ethical Sources

**Public company information:**
- Website, pricing page, changelog, blog, help docs
- Press releases and news coverage
- SEC filings (public companies)
- Job postings (reveal priorities and tech stack)

**User-generated content:**
- G2, Capterra, TrustRadius reviews (patterns in complaints and praise)
- Reddit, Twitter/X, LinkedIn discussions
- Community forums and Slack groups
- Stack Overflow and developer forums (for technical products)

**Industry and analyst content:**
- Analyst reports (Gartner, Forrester, if available)
- Industry conference talks and slides
- Podcasts featuring competitor leaders
- Trade publication articles

**Direct experience:**
- Free trials and freemium tiers (use their product)
- Attend their webinars and demos
- Subscribe to their email newsletters
- Follow their social channels

**Your own data:**
- Win/loss interviews with prospects who considered competitors
- Customer feedback mentioning competitors
- Sales team intelligence from deal cycles
- Support tickets referencing competitor features

### What's Off-Limits

- Misrepresenting yourself to access private information
- Using leaked confidential documents
- Reverse engineering proprietary algorithms
- Scraping gated or private content
- Pressuring employees of competitors for inside information

## Competitive Analysis Frameworks

### Feature Comparison Matrix

| Feature | You | Competitor A | Competitor B | Competitor C |
|---------|-----|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| [Feature 1] | ✅ Full | ✅ Full | ⚡ Partial | ❌ No |
| [Feature 2] | ⚡ Partial | ✅ Full | ✅ Full | ⚡ Partial |
| [Feature 3] | ✅ Full | ❌ No | ⚡ Partial | ✅ Full |
| **Pricing** | $X/mo | $Y/mo | Free | $Z/mo |

**Rules:**
- Only compare features that matter to users (not internal capabilities)
- Be honest about your gaps — internal audiences need truth, not spin
- Update quarterly — features change fast
- Weight features by importance to your target segment

### Positioning Map

Plot competitors on two dimensions that matter to your users.

**Common axis pairs:**
- Price vs. Functionality
- Ease of use vs. Power/Flexibility
- SMB-focused vs. Enterprise-focused
- Generic vs. Specialized

**How to use it:**
- Identify whitespace (underserved positions)
- Validate your positioning (are you where you think you are?)
- Track movement over time (competitors shifting position)

### Win/Loss Analysis

After every lost deal (and a sample of wins), capture:

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Who did we lose/win against? | [Competitor name] |
| What was the deciding factor? | [Price, feature, trust, timing, etc.] |
| What did they say about us? | [Strengths and weaknesses mentioned] |
| What did they say about the competitor? | [What appealed to them] |
| Was there anything we could have done differently? | [Tactical learning] |

**Aggregate insights quarterly:**
- Top 3 reasons we win
- Top 3 reasons we lose
- Features most frequently cited as gaps
- Competitor most frequently encountered

## Tracking and Update Cadence

### Recommended Schedule

| Activity | Frequency | Owner |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Review competitor websites/changelogs | Monthly | Product Marketing |
| Update feature comparison matrix | Quarterly | Product |
| Win/loss analysis summary | Quarterly | Sales + Product Marketing |
| Full competitive landscape review | Bi-annually | Strategy/Product |
| Deep-dive on specific competitor | Ad hoc (trigger-based) | Product |

### Triggers for Ad Hoc Deep-Dives

- Competitor raises significant funding
- Competitor launches a feature that overlaps with your roadmap
- You're losing deals to a competitor you previously beat
- A competitor makes a major acquisition or partnership
- A new entrant appears in your space

## Turning Intel into Strategy

### From Data to Decision

1. **Observe:** Competitor launched [feature/change]
2. **Interpret:** This suggests they're targeting [segment/use case]
3. **Assess impact:** This affects us because [reason]
4. **Decide:** Our response is [action] / We choose not to respond because [reason]

### Response Options

| Response | When to Use |
|----------|------------|
| **Ignore** | Their move doesn't affect your target segment |
| **Monitor** | Potentially relevant but not urgent |
| **Differentiate** | Double down on what makes you different |
| **Match** | Table-stakes feature your users expect |
| **Leapfrog** | You can build a significantly better version |
| **Reposition** | The competitive landscape has fundamentally shifted |

### Anti-Patterns

- **Reactive roadmapping:** Building everything competitors build. You lose your identity.
- **Competitor worship:** Assuming they know something you don't. They're guessing too.
- **Analysis paralysis:** Tracking 30 competitors in detail. Focus on 3-5 that matter.
- **Ignoring substitutes:** Only watching direct competitors while users switch to entirely different solutions.
