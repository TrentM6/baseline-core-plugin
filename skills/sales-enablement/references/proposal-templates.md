# Proposal Templates

Structures for proposals, SOWs, and pricing presentation. Load this when creating formal sales documents.

---

## Proposal Structure

A proposal is not a product pitch. It's a document that says: "I understand your problem, here's how I'll solve it, and here's what it costs." The prospect should see themselves in every section.

### Standard Proposal

**1. Executive Summary** (1 paragraph)
- What the prospect needs, what you're proposing, and the expected outcome
- Write this last, but put it first
- No jargon, no feature lists — just the essence

**2. Understanding of the Problem** (half page)
- Restate the prospect's challenge in their words
- Reference specific things they told you in discovery
- Show that you understand their world, not just their requirements
- This section builds trust — if you get the problem right, they trust your solution

**3. Proposed Solution** (1-2 pages)
- What you'll do, how you'll do it, and why this approach
- Connect every element back to the problem stated above
- Use phases or milestones to make it feel structured and manageable
- Include what's in scope AND what's out of scope

**4. Timeline & Milestones**
- Visual timeline if possible (even a simple table works)
- Key milestones with dates or relative timing
- Dependencies and what you need from the prospect
- Don't over-promise on timing — realistic timelines build trust

**5. Investment** (see Pricing Presentation below)
- Present the price with context and confidence
- Tie it back to value, not just deliverables
- Include payment terms

**6. About Us** (half page max)
- Keep it brief — they already know who you are
- Focus on relevant experience and proof points
- Include 1-2 brief case studies that mirror their situation

**7. Next Steps**
- Exactly what happens if they say yes
- Remove friction — make it easy to move forward
- Include a timeline for decision (soft deadline)

## SOW (Statement of Work)

A SOW is the operational companion to a proposal. It's what you work from. More detailed, more structured, less narrative.

### SOW Structure

**1. Overview**
- Project name, parties, effective date
- One-paragraph project description

**2. Scope of Work**
- Detailed description of deliverables
- Organized by phase or workstream
- Each item should be specific enough to verify completion
- Acceptance criteria for key deliverables

**3. Out of Scope**
- Explicitly state what's not included
- This section prevents scope creep and protects both parties

**4. Timeline**

| Phase | Deliverable | Duration | Start | End |
|-------|------------|----------|-------|-----|
| Phase 1 | [Deliverable] | X weeks | [Date] | [Date] |
| Phase 2 | [Deliverable] | X weeks | [Date] | [Date] |

**5. Roles & Responsibilities**

| Role | Responsibility | Party |
|------|---------------|-------|
| Project Lead | [Description] | [You/Client] |
| Stakeholder | [Description] | [Client] |

**6. Investment & Payment Terms**
- Total project cost
- Payment schedule (milestone-based, monthly, upfront)
- What triggers each payment
- Late payment terms

**7. Assumptions & Dependencies**
- What you're assuming to be true
- What you need from the client to stay on track
- What happens if assumptions change

**8. Change Management**
- How changes to scope are requested and approved
- Impact on timeline and cost
- Who has authority to approve changes

**9. Signatures**
- Signature blocks for both parties
- Date fields

## Pricing Presentation

How you present pricing matters as much as the price itself.

### Principles

1. **Never lead with the number.** Build value first — the price should feel reasonable by the time they see it.
2. **Anchor high.** If you have tiers, show the highest first. If you're presenting one option, mention the cost of not solving the problem.
3. **Tie price to value, not hours.** "This project will reduce your churn by X%" not "This is 200 hours at $Y/hour."
4. **Present options when possible.** Three options (basic, recommended, premium) let the prospect choose instead of saying yes/no.
5. **Be confident.** Hesitation around pricing signals that you don't believe in your value.

### Three-Tier Pricing Table

| | Essential | Recommended | Premium |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Includes** | [Core deliverables] | [Core + additional] | [Everything + extras] |
| **Timeline** | X weeks | X weeks | X weeks |
| **Support** | [Level] | [Level] | [Level] |
| **Investment** | $X | $X | $X |

- Label the middle option "Recommended" — it's where most prospects land
- Each tier should make sense on its own, not feel like an upsell trap
- The top tier should include things that are genuinely valuable, not padding

### Single-Price Presentation

When you're presenting one option:

> **Investment: $X**
>
> This includes [brief summary of what's included]. Based on [specific value driver from their situation], this represents [value context — ROI, cost savings, revenue impact].
>
> **Payment:** [Terms]

### Pricing Objection Responses

| Objection | Response approach |
|-----------|------------------|
| "That's more than we expected" | Revisit the problem cost. "What's the cost of not solving this?" |
| "Can you break down the hours?" | Redirect to value. "We price based on outcomes, not hours. Here's why..." |
| "Competitor X is cheaper" | Acknowledge, differentiate. "They may be — here's what's different about our approach..." |
| "We need to think about it" | Add a timeline. "Of course. When would you like to reconnect? I want to make sure we can hold your timeline." |
| "Can you do it for less?" | Adjust scope, not price. "We can — here's what we'd remove to hit that number." |

## Proposal Anti-Patterns

| Don't | Do |
|-------|-----|
| Lead with your company history | Lead with their problem |
| Include every feature you offer | Include only what's relevant to them |
| Use generic case studies | Use case studies that mirror their situation |
| Hide the price at the end | Present pricing with context and confidence |
| Write 20 pages | Write as short as possible — respect their time |
| Send and wait | Send and follow up with a specific next step |
| Copy-paste from your last proposal | Customize every section for this prospect |
