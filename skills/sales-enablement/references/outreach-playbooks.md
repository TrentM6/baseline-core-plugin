# Outreach Playbooks

Cold outreach, follow-up sequences, and demo scripts. Load this when creating sales communication designed to start or advance a conversation.

---

## Cold Outreach

### Email

**The formula:** Relevance → Problem → Proof → Ask

Every cold email must answer three questions in the first 3 seconds:
1. Why are you emailing me specifically?
2. What's in it for me?
3. What do you want me to do?

**Structure:**
```
Subject: [Specific, relevant, under 50 characters]

[1 sentence: Why you, why now — show you've done your homework]

[1-2 sentences: The problem you solve, stated in their terms]

[1 sentence: Proof — a specific result with a similar company]

[1 sentence: The ask — specific, low-commitment]

[Signature]
```

**Example pattern:**
```
Subject: [Their company]'s [specific area] — quick question

Hi [Name],

I noticed [specific thing about their company — recent hire, product launch,
public metric, content they published]. [Connect it to a challenge you solve.]

We work with [category of companies like theirs] on [problem you solve].
[One specific result: "helped [Company] achieve [metric] in [timeframe]."]

Would a 15-minute call next week make sense to see if this is relevant?

[Signature]
```

**Rules:**
- Subject line: 3-7 words, no caps lock, no exclamation marks
- Personalize the first sentence — it proves you're not mass-emailing
- One proof point, not three. Specific beats comprehensive.
- The ask is a meeting, not a purchase. Match the commitment to the relationship.
- Under 100 words total. Shorter emails get more replies.
- No attachments in cold emails. No links in the first email if possible.

### LinkedIn Message

**Structure:** Connection request note (short) → Follow-up message (if accepted)

**Connection request (300 char limit):**
```
Hi [Name] — I work with [category] on [problem]. Saw your [specific thing]
and thought it'd be worth connecting. No pitch — just relevant work.
```

**Follow-up after connection:**
```
Thanks for connecting, [Name].

I noticed [observation about their work/company]. We've been helping
[similar companies] with [specific problem] — [one result].

Would it be useful to swap notes on [topic]? Happy to share what
we've been seeing.
```

**Rules:**
- Don't pitch in the connection request
- Wait 1-2 days after they accept before messaging
- Reference something specific about them
- Offer value, not just a meeting

## Follow-Up Sequences

### After No Response to Cold Outreach

**Email 2 (3-4 days after Email 1):**
- Short. Add a new angle or piece of value.
- Don't say "just following up" or "circling back"
- Pattern: "One more thought on [topic] — [new insight or relevant content]"

**Email 3 (5-7 days after Email 2):**
- Shift angle. Try a different pain point or approach.
- Consider sharing a relevant resource (article, case study, data point)
- Pattern: "I wrote about [relevant topic] and thought of [their situation]"

**Email 4 — The Breakup (7-10 days after Email 3):**
- Be direct: "I don't want to clutter your inbox"
- Give them an easy out or an easy in
- Pattern: "Should I stop reaching out, or is the timing just off?"

**Rules:**
- Never more than 4-5 emails in a cold sequence
- Each email must add new value — don't just re-send the same message
- Space them out. Desperation is obvious.
- Track opens and replies to adjust timing

### After a Meeting / Demo

**Same-day follow-up:**
- Thank them. Recap the 2-3 key takeaways.
- Attach or link anything you promised.
- Confirm the next step with a specific date/time.

**Pattern:**
```
Subject: Next steps from our conversation

Hi [Name],

Thanks for the time today. Here's what I took away:

- [Key point 1 — their problem in their words]
- [Key point 2 — how you'd approach it]
- [Key point 3 — agreed next step]

[Attached/linked: whatever you promised to send]

I'll [specific next action] by [specific date]. Let me know if I
missed anything or if your thinking has shifted.

[Signature]
```

**If no response after meeting follow-up (3-5 days):**
- Add value: share a relevant case study or insight
- Restate the next step you agreed on
- Don't be passive — "Wanted to make sure this didn't get buried"

**If still no response (7-10 days):**
- Acknowledge the silence directly
- Make it easy to respond: "Is this still a priority, or has timing shifted?"
- Offer a clear out: "No worries either way — just want to respect your time"

## Demo Scripts

### Pre-Demo Preparation

Before every demo, answer these questions:
1. What is the prospect's #1 problem?
2. What have they tried before?
3. Who will be in the room? What do they care about?
4. What's the one thing that would make them say "we need this"?
5. What objections are likely?

### Demo Structure

**1. Confirm the agenda (2 min)**
- "Here's what I was planning to cover based on our last conversation. Does this still make sense, or has anything changed?"
- Let them adjust — it shows you're flexible and attentive

**2. Recap their problem (3 min)**
- Restate their challenge in their words
- Confirm you've got it right: "Is that a fair summary?"
- This builds trust and ensures you're demo-ing the right thing

**3. Show the solution (10-15 min)**
- Only show what's relevant to their problem
- For each feature: "You mentioned [problem]. Here's how this handles that."
- Use their data or scenarios if possible (or realistic examples from their industry)
- Pause for questions. Don't barrel through slides.

**4. Proof (3-5 min)**
- "Companies like [similar company] were dealing with the same thing. Here's what happened."
- One story, told well, beats five bullet points

**5. Q&A and objections (5-10 min)**
- Welcome questions: "What concerns do you have?"
- Don't be defensive. Acknowledge, then address.
- If you don't know the answer: "I want to give you an accurate answer — let me follow up on that by [date]."

**6. Next steps (2 min)**
- Be specific: "Based on what we discussed, here's what I'd suggest as a next step."
- Propose a date and time for the next conversation
- Clarify who needs to be involved in the decision

### Demo Anti-Patterns

| Don't | Do |
|-------|-----|
| Show every feature | Show only what's relevant to their problem |
| Talk for 30 minutes straight | Pause every 3-5 minutes for questions |
| Use generic sample data | Use their industry or realistic scenarios |
| Skip the problem recap | Confirm you understand before showing solutions |
| End with "any questions?" | End with a specific next step |
| Demo to the wrong audience | Confirm attendees and their priorities in advance |

## Objection Handling

### Framework: Acknowledge → Explore → Respond → Confirm

1. **Acknowledge:** "That's a fair concern." Never dismiss an objection.
2. **Explore:** "Can you tell me more about what's behind that?" Get to the real issue.
3. **Respond:** Address with evidence, not argument.
4. **Confirm:** "Does that help? What else is on your mind?"

### Common Objections

| Objection | What they're really saying | Response approach |
|-----------|--------------------------|-------------------|
| "We don't have the budget" | It's not a priority yet | Revisit the cost of the problem. Help them build the business case. |
| "We're happy with our current solution" | Switching costs feel high | Acknowledge what works, surface what doesn't. "What would need to change for you to consider alternatives?" |
| "We need to think about it" | Something is unresolved | "Of course. What would help you make that decision? Is there information I can provide?" |
| "The timing isn't right" | Could be real or a soft no | "When would be a better time? I'm happy to reconnect in [timeframe]." |
| "We need to get buy-in from [person]" | There's another decision-maker | "Can I help prepare materials for that conversation? What does [person] care about most?" |
| "Your competitor does X that you don't" | They're comparing features | "You're right — they do. Here's why we chose a different approach, and what that means for [their specific situation]." |
| "It's too expensive" | Value isn't clear enough | Revisit ROI. "If this saves you X hours per week, that's $Y per year. The investment pays for itself in [timeframe]." |

## Outreach Anti-Patterns

| Don't | Do |
|-------|-----|
| "Just checking in" | Add value with every touchpoint |
| Mass-email with mail merge tokens showing | Personalize meaningfully or don't send |
| Follow up daily | Space outreach 3-7 days apart |
| Send a 500-word cold email | Under 100 words. Respect their time. |
| Pitch in a LinkedIn connection request | Connect first, add value, then explore fit |
| Ignore objections | Acknowledge, explore, respond with evidence |
| Give up after one email | Follow up 3-4 times with new angles |
| Be pushy or guilt-trip | Be persistent and professional — there's a difference |
