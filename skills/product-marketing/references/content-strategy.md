# Content Strategy

> Planning, creating, and measuring content that supports product marketing goals — pillars, funnel mapping, editorial planning, and performance.

**Part of:** [Product Marketing](../product-marketing-skill.md)

## Content Pillars

### What They Are

Content pillars are 3-5 core themes that all content maps back to. They connect your positioning to your audience's interests.

### How to Define Them

1. **Start with your positioning pillars** (from your messaging framework)
2. **Map each to audience interests** — What does your target audience search for, read, and share?
3. **Validate with data** — Search volume, competitor content gaps, customer questions
4. **Name them clearly** — Each pillar should be a topic area anyone on the team understands

### Example

For a product analytics platform:

| Pillar | Positioning It Supports | Audience Interest |
|--------|------------------------|-------------------|
| Data-driven product decisions | "Make better decisions faster" | How to use data in product management |
| Experimentation culture | "Test everything, assume nothing" | A/B testing, feature flags, experiment design |
| Metrics that matter | "Focus on signals, ignore noise" | North Star metrics, avoiding vanity metrics |
| Product-led growth | "Let the product do the selling" | Activation, onboarding, conversion optimization |

### Rules

- Every piece of content should map to a pillar. If it doesn't fit, it's off-strategy.
- Pillars should be broad enough to generate 20+ content ideas each, but specific enough to be ownable.
- Review and refine pillars annually or when positioning changes.

## Content-to-Funnel Mapping

### Content by Buyer Stage

| Stage | Audience State | Content Goal | Content Types |
|-------|---------------|-------------|---------------|
| **Awareness** | Knows the problem, not the solution | Educate, build trust | Blog posts, social content, podcasts, industry reports, thought leadership |
| **Consideration** | Evaluating solutions | Differentiate, demonstrate value | Comparison guides, webinars, case studies, product deep-dives, demo videos |
| **Decision** | Ready to buy, choosing vendor | Convert, remove risk | Free trials, ROI calculators, customer stories, implementation guides, pricing pages |
| **Retention** | Existing customer | Expand usage, reduce churn | Product tips, advanced guides, community content, release notes, success stories |

### Mapping Rules

- **Top-of-funnel content should NOT sell.** It earns attention by being genuinely useful.
- **Mid-funnel content should show, not just tell.** Demos, case studies, comparisons — let them evaluate.
- **Bottom-funnel content should remove friction.** Answer every objection and make the path to purchase clear.
- **Retention content is often neglected.** Invest in it — keeping customers is cheaper than acquiring new ones.

## Editorial Calendar Design

### Calendar Structure

| Week | Pillar | Content Type | Title/Topic | Channel | Owner | Status |
|------|--------|-------------|-------------|---------|-------|--------|
| W1 | Metrics | Blog post | [Title] | Blog, LinkedIn, Email | [Name] | Draft |
| W2 | Experimentation | Webinar | [Title] | Live event, YouTube | [Name] | Planned |
| W3 | PLG | Case study | [Title] | Blog, Sales deck | [Name] | In review |

### Cadence Guidelines

| Content Type | Typical Cadence | Notes |
|-------------|----------------|-------|
| Blog posts | 1-4x/month | Consistency matters more than volume |
| Social media | 3-5x/week | Mix original content with engagement |
| Newsletter | 1-2x/month | Curated value, not just announcements |
| Webinars | 1x/month | Quality over quantity |
| Case studies | 1-2x/quarter | Gate behind lead form or publish freely based on strategy |
| Product updates | Per release | Can be part of blog or standalone changelog |

### Planning Process

1. **Quarterly:** Set themes based on product roadmap, campaigns, and market events
2. **Monthly:** Plan specific pieces, assign owners, set deadlines
3. **Weekly:** Review status, adjust based on what's performing or what's urgent
4. **Daily:** Publish, promote, engage with responses

## Content Repurposing Strategy

### One → Many Framework

Start with one substantial piece, then extract derivatives:

```
Original: Long-form research report or webinar

Derivatives:
├── Blog post (key findings summary)
├── LinkedIn post series (one insight per post)
├── Twitter/X thread (data highlights)
├── Infographic (visual summary)
├── Email newsletter (curated excerpt)
├── Slide deck (for sales team)
├── Video clip (key 2-minute segment)
└── Quote cards (for social media)
```

### Repurposing Rules

- Adapt for the channel, don't just copy-paste. Each channel has different norms.
- Change the format but keep the core insight. The same finding can be a chart, a quote, a story, or a stat.
- Stagger the timing. Don't publish all derivatives on the same day.
- Track which derivative format performs best per channel.

## SEO Fundamentals for Product Marketers

### Keyword Intent

| Intent Type | What They're Doing | Content to Create |
|------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Informational** | Learning about a topic | How-to guides, explainer blog posts |
| **Navigational** | Looking for a specific brand/tool | Landing pages, product pages |
| **Commercial** | Researching before buying | Comparison pages, reviews, "best [category]" posts |
| **Transactional** | Ready to buy or sign up | Pricing pages, free trial pages, demo requests |

### Topic Clusters

**Hub and spoke model:**

```
           ┌── Spoke: "How to calculate LTV"
           │
Hub: "SaaS ├── Spoke: "LTV benchmarks by industry"
 Metrics   │
 Guide"    ├── Spoke: "LTV vs. CAC explained"
           │
           └── Spoke: "Tools for tracking LTV"
```

- **Hub page:** Comprehensive guide covering the broad topic
- **Spoke pages:** Detailed posts covering specific subtopics
- **Internal links:** Each spoke links back to the hub, hub links to all spokes
- **Benefit:** Establishes topical authority with search engines

### Search-Driven Content Process

1. **Research keywords** relevant to your pillars
2. **Assess intent** — match content format to what the searcher wants
3. **Check competition** — can you create something meaningfully better?
4. **Create content** that answers the query better than existing results
5. **Optimize on-page** — title, headings, meta description, internal links
6. **Promote and build links** — social, email, outreach
7. **Monitor and refresh** — update rankings and traffic quarterly

## Content Performance Measurement

### Metrics by Content Type

| Content Type | Primary Metrics | Secondary Metrics |
|-------------|----------------|-------------------|
| Blog posts | Organic traffic, time on page | Social shares, backlinks, lead gen |
| Webinars | Registrations, attendance rate | Engagement score, MQLs generated |
| Case studies | Page views, PDF downloads | Deal influence, sales usage |
| Social media | Engagement rate, follower growth | Click-through rate, share of voice |
| Email/newsletter | Open rate, click rate | Unsubscribe rate, conversion |
| Video | Views, watch time | Engagement, click-through |

### What "Good" Looks Like

| Metric | Benchmark | Context |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Blog traffic growth | 10-20% month-over-month | For early content programs |
| Email open rate | 20-30% | B2B benchmark |
| Email click rate | 2-5% | B2B benchmark |
| Webinar attendance rate | 40-50% of registrations | Average is 40% |
| Social engagement rate | 1-3% | Varies by platform |

### Attribution

Content rarely drives a purchase directly. Track:
- **First touch:** What content started the relationship?
- **Last touch:** What content preceded the conversion?
- **Multi-touch:** Which content was consumed along the journey?
- **Deal influence:** Which content appeared in the buyer's journey for closed deals?

## Content Audit Methodology

### Step 1: Inventory

List every piece of content with:
- URL / location
- Title
- Content type (blog, video, landing page, etc.)
- Publication date
- Content pillar (if it maps to one)
- Performance data (traffic, conversions, engagement)

### Step 2: Evaluate

Score each piece on:
- **Performance:** Is it driving traffic, leads, or engagement?
- **Relevance:** Is it still accurate and aligned with current positioning?
- **Quality:** Does it meet current standards?

### Step 3: Decide

| Category | Criteria | Action |
|----------|----------|--------|
| **Keep** | Performing well, still relevant | No action needed |
| **Update** | Good topic, but outdated or underperforming | Refresh content, update data, re-promote |
| **Consolidate** | Multiple thin pieces on similar topics | Merge into one comprehensive piece |
| **Remove** | Outdated, off-brand, or irrelevant | Redirect URL, remove from site |

### Step 4: Execute

- Prioritize updates by potential impact (high-traffic pages first)
- Set redirect rules for removed content
- Track performance of updated content vs. baseline

## Thought Leadership Strategy

### Building Authority

**Thought leadership is NOT:**
- Rewriting what everyone else says
- Making big claims without evidence
- Self-promotional content disguised as insight

**Thought leadership IS:**
- Original perspectives grounded in experience or data
- Challenging conventional wisdom with evidence
- Sharing methodologies, frameworks, or approaches others can learn from
- Being consistently present on a specific topic over time

### Approaches

| Approach | How It Works | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **Original research** | Conduct studies, publish findings | Annual industry report, benchmarking data |
| **Practitioner insight** | Share lessons from real work | "What we learned launching 50 products" |
| **Contrarian view** | Challenge accepted wisdom with evidence | "Why NPS is the wrong metric for SaaS" |
| **Framework creation** | Develop reusable approaches | "Our 3-step product validation method" |
| **Curation + commentary** | Aggregate and interpret trends | Weekly roundup with expert perspective |

### Consistency

Thought leadership is a long game. Publishing one great piece matters less than showing up consistently:
- Pick a cadence you can sustain (weekly, bi-weekly, monthly)
- Pick 1-2 channels to focus on (don't spread thin)
- Build a backlog of ideas so you never start from zero
- Engage with others in your space — thought leadership is a conversation
