# Product Marketing

Product marketing is the discipline of making products compelling to the right audience. It bridges product and market — translating what the product does into why it matters, for whom, and how to reach them.

## Core Principles

1. **Positioning before messaging.** Know who you're for and what you stand for before writing a word.
2. **Customer language, not product language.** Describe benefits in their words, not yours.
3. **Evidence over claims.** Every claim needs a proof point. Show, don't just tell.
4. **Specificity wins.** "Saves 10 hours/week" beats "improves efficiency."
5. **Consistency builds trust.** Same positioning, voice, and proof points across all channels.
6. **Test and iterate.** Marketing is hypothesis-driven. Measure what resonates.

## Workflow

### 1. Clarify Before Starting
- What's the goal (awareness, conversion, retention)?
- Who is the audience (persona, stage, context)?
- What's the channel (LinkedIn, email, website, deck)?
- What proof points exist?
- What's the competitive context?

### 2. Choose the Right Approach

| Need | Approach |
|------|----------|
| Define who we're for | Positioning exercise |
| Craft core messaging | Messaging framework |
| Create content | Content with messaging framework |
| Launch a feature | Launch brief + messaging |
| Competitive response | Competitive messaging |
| Prove value | Case study / proof points |

### 3. Marketing Process

**Position:** Define audience > identify key problem > articulate unique value > differentiate from alternatives.

**Message:** Develop messaging hierarchy > create proof points > test with audience > refine.

**Create:** Apply messaging to channel > use voice guidelines > include proof points > strong CTA.

**Measure:** Track performance > identify what resonates > iterate messaging.

## Quality Checks

- [ ] Clear target audience defined?
- [ ] Positioning is specific and differentiated?
- [ ] Customer language, not product jargon?
- [ ] Claims supported by evidence?
- [ ] Consistent with brand voice?
- [ ] Clear call to action?
- [ ] Channel-appropriate format?

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Instead |
|--------------|---------|
| Feature lists instead of benefits | Lead with outcomes |
| Generic messaging | Specific to audience and context |
| Claims without proof | Every claim needs evidence |
| Inconsistent voice | Follow voice guidelines |
| No clear CTA | Every piece needs a next step |
