# Project Management

Project management is the discipline of turning strategy into shipped work. It's about creating the conditions for teams to execute effectively — clear priorities, realistic timelines, managed risks, and honest communication.

## Core Principles

1. **Plans are hypotheses.** They'll change. Build in flexibility from the start.
2. **Transparency over comfort.** Bad news early is better than bad news late.
3. **Outcomes over output.** Shipping features isn't success. Delivering value is.
4. **Remove blockers, don't manage tasks.** Your job is to clear the path.
5. **Communicate proactively.** Stakeholders should never be surprised.
6. **Right-size the process.** Don't impose enterprise process on a 3-person team.
7. **Retrospect relentlessly.** Every project has lessons. Capture them.

## Workflow

### 1. Clarify Before Starting
- What's the goal and success criteria?
- What are the constraints (time, people, budget)?
- Who are the stakeholders?
- What are the known risks?
- What's the team's working style?

### 2. Choose the Right Approach

| Situation | Approach |
|-----------|----------|
| New initiative | Project kickoff, scope definition, milestone planning |
| Ongoing work | Sprint planning, backlog management |
| Status communication | Status reports, stakeholder updates |
| Things going wrong | Risk management, re-planning |
| Project completion | Retrospective, lessons learned |

### 3. Project Process

**Plan:** Define scope > break into milestones > identify risks > assign ownership > set cadence.

**Execute:** Run sprints/iterations > track progress > manage blockers > communicate status.

**Adapt:** Monitor risks > adjust plan > communicate changes > protect scope.

**Close:** Retrospective > document lessons > celebrate wins > hand off.

## Quality Checks

- [ ] Goals and success criteria clear?
- [ ] Risks identified and owned?
- [ ] Stakeholders informed and aligned?
- [ ] Team has what they need?
- [ ] Status is transparent?
- [ ] Process matches team size?
- [ ] Retrospective scheduled?

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Instead |
|--------------|---------|
| Plans as promises | Plans as hypotheses, updated regularly |
| Status theater | Honest, transparent communication |
| Process for process' sake | Right-size to the team |
| Surprise bad news | Communicate risks early |
| No retrospective | Always close the loop |
