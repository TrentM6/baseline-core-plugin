# Change Management

> Frameworks for driving adoption, managing resistance, and sustaining change. Applicable to project management, go-to-market, and skill building.

---

## Adoption Curves

### The Technology Adoption Lifecycle

```
   Innovators → Early Adopters → Early Majority → Late Majority → Laggards
     (2.5%)       (13.5%)          (34%)           (34%)          (16%)
```

**Innovators:** Will try anything new. Seek them out for early feedback and prototyping.

**Early Adopters:** Visionaries who see strategic value. Your best champions. They'll tolerate rough edges if the vision is compelling.

**Early Majority:** Pragmatists who want proven solutions. Need evidence, references, and low-risk onboarding.

**Late Majority:** Skeptics who adopt only when it's the norm. Need peer pressure, mandate, or overwhelming evidence.

**Laggards:** Resist until the old way is no longer available. Don't optimize for them early — focus on the majority.

### Crossing the Chasm

The hardest gap is between Early Adopters and Early Majority. What works for visionaries (vision, potential) doesn't work for pragmatists (proof, stability).

**To cross:**
- Build reference cases from early adopter success
- Reduce implementation risk (templates, training, support)
- Show ROI in pragmatist terms (efficiency, cost savings, risk reduction)
- Make the new way easier than the old way

## Resistance Patterns

### Why People Resist Change

| Root Cause | What It Sounds Like | What's Really Going On |
|-----------|---------------------|----------------------|
| **Loss of control** | "Nobody asked me about this" | They feel change is happening to them, not with them |
| **Uncertainty** | "What does this mean for my role?" | They can't see themselves in the future state |
| **Competence threat** | "I'm fine with how I do it now" | They're worried about looking incompetent |
| **Bad timing** | "We have too much going on already" | Change fatigue, competing priorities |
| **Past failures** | "We tried something like this before" | Burned by previous change initiatives |
| **Misaligned incentives** | "This doesn't help me hit my goals" | The change doesn't align with how they're measured |

### Diagnosing Resistance

**Ask these questions:**
1. Who is resisting? (Individuals, teams, leadership?)
2. What specifically are they resisting? (The change itself, the process, the timing?)
3. Is the resistance rational? (Do they have a valid point?)
4. What would need to be true for them to support this?

**Key principle:** Resistance is information. It tells you what's not working about your change approach. Listen to it before trying to overcome it.

## Kotter's 8-Step Model (Practical Version)

### The Steps

**Phase 1: Create a climate for change**

**1. Create urgency:**
- Why change, why now?
- What's the cost of staying the same?
- Use data, stories, competitive pressure
- *Don't manufacture fake urgency — people see through it*

**2. Build a guiding coalition:**
- Identify 3-5 influential people across the organization
- They don't need to be senior — they need to be respected
- Give them ownership and voice in shaping the change
- *This is your change leadership team, not just your approval chain*

**3. Form a strategic vision:**
- What does the future state look like? (Specific and vivid)
- How is it better than today? (For the people affected, not just the organization)
- Can you explain it in 30 seconds? (If not, simplify)

**Phase 2: Engage and enable the organization**

**4. Communicate the vision:**
- Say it 7 times in 7 ways (email, meeting, 1:1, demo, doc, Slack, all-hands)
- Address "what's in it for me?" for each audience
- Listen as much as you broadcast — communication is two-way
- *Under-communication is the #1 reason change initiatives fail*

**5. Remove barriers:**
- Identify what makes the new way harder than the old way
- Fix systems, tools, processes that create friction
- Address people who actively block (coaching, escalation)
- *Don't just tell people to change — make it easy to change*

**6. Generate short-term wins:**
- Plan for visible, meaningful results within 30-90 days
- Celebrate and publicize wins broadly
- Use wins to build momentum and credibility
- *Long timelines without visible progress kill change initiatives*

**Phase 3: Sustain the change**

**7. Build on gains:**
- Don't declare victory too early
- Use credibility from wins to tackle harder changes
- Keep pushing — the middle is where most change stalls
- *"Good enough" is the enemy of lasting change*

**8. Anchor in culture:**
- Connect the change to organizational values
- Embed new practices into hiring, onboarding, performance reviews
- Tell stories about the new way succeeding
- *Change is complete when people do it without thinking about it*

## ADKAR Model

### The Five Elements

**A — Awareness:** Does the person understand why the change is happening?
- If no: communicate the why (business case, customer impact, competitive pressure)
- Common failure: assuming people know why

**D — Desire:** Does the person want to support the change?
- If no: address what's in it for them, listen to concerns, involve them
- Common failure: mandating change without building buy-in

**K — Knowledge:** Does the person know how to change?
- If no: training, documentation, mentoring, examples
- Common failure: expecting people to figure it out

**A — Ability:** Can the person actually perform the new behavior?
- If no: practice time, coaching, tool access, reduced workload during transition
- Common failure: training without practice opportunity

**R — Reinforcement:** Are there systems keeping the change in place?
- If no: recognition, metrics, accountability, removal of old systems
- Common failure: declaring victory and moving on

### Using ADKAR for Diagnosis

When someone isn't adopting the change, diagnose which element is missing:

| Behavior | Likely ADKAR Gap |
|----------|-----------------|
| "Why are we doing this?" | Awareness |
| "I don't see how this helps me" | Desire |
| "I don't know how to do this" | Knowledge |
| "I tried but can't make it work" | Ability |
| "I did it once but went back to the old way" | Reinforcement |

**Rule:** Address gaps in order. There's no point training someone (Knowledge) who doesn't understand why the change matters (Awareness) or doesn't want to do it (Desire).

## Communication for Change

### Frequency and Transparency

- **Early phase:** Communicate 2-3x more than you think necessary
- **During transition:** Weekly updates minimum, daily for major changes
- **Steady state:** Regular check-ins, reducing as the change stabilizes

### Message Framework by Phase

| Phase | Key Message | Tone |
|-------|------------|------|
| **Announcement** | What's changing, why, and what's next | Clear, honest, forward-looking |
| **Planning** | How we'll get there, what support is available | Collaborative, inclusive |
| **Implementation** | What's happening this week, early results | Action-oriented, celebratory |
| **Stabilization** | What's working, what we're adjusting | Reflective, appreciative |

### Addressing Concerns

**Do:**
- Acknowledge uncertainty: "We don't have all the answers yet, and here's how we'll figure it out"
- Be specific about impact: "Here's what changes for your role and here's what doesn't"
- Provide a channel for questions: "Drop questions in [channel] and we'll answer within 24 hours"

**Don't:**
- Promise things you can't guarantee: "Nothing will change for you" (if it might)
- Dismiss concerns: "Trust me, it'll be fine" (they won't trust you)
- Over-communicate positivity: "This is going to be amazing!" (sounds out of touch)

## Training and Enablement

### Progressive Skill Building

**Don't dump everything at once.** Build skills progressively:

| Week | Focus | Activity |
|------|-------|----------|
| 1 | Awareness + basics | Overview session, getting started guide |
| 2 | Core workflows | Hands-on workshop, practice exercises |
| 3 | Advanced features | Deep-dive session, Q&A |
| 4 | Independent practice | Office hours, peer support |
| Ongoing | Mastery | Tips, advanced guides, community |

### Support Structures

| Support Type | When to Use | Format |
|-------------|------------|--------|
| **Documentation** | Self-service, reference | How-to guides, FAQ, video tutorials |
| **Training sessions** | Initial rollout | Live workshop (in-person or virtual) |
| **Office hours** | Ongoing support | Weekly drop-in session |
| **Champions/ambassadors** | Peer support | Trained super-users on each team |
| **1:1 coaching** | Individuals struggling | Targeted help for specific blockers |
| **Community/forum** | Peer learning | Slack channel, internal forum |

### Enablement Anti-Patterns

- **Training dump:** 3-hour session covering everything. Nobody retains it.
- **No practice time:** Training without time to apply it. Skills decay immediately.
- **One-size-fits-all:** Same training for power users and beginners.
- **No follow-up:** Training event with no ongoing support.

## Measuring Adoption

### Leading vs. Lagging Indicators

**Leading indicators** (predict future adoption):
- Training completion rate
- Tool login frequency (in the first 30 days)
- Support ticket topics (are they about the new system?)
- Champion activity (are champions advocating?)
- Sentiment survey results

**Lagging indicators** (confirm adoption happened):
- Usage of old system (should decline)
- Usage of new system (should match expected levels)
- Business outcomes (the metrics the change was supposed to improve)
- Employee satisfaction / engagement scores
- Time-to-competency (how long until new users are productive)

### Adoption Metrics Framework

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|------------|
| **Awareness** | 100% of affected people know about the change | Survey or quiz |
| **Training completion** | 90%+ within first month | LMS or attendance records |
| **Active usage** | 80%+ using new system within 60 days | System analytics |
| **Proficiency** | 70%+ self-report confidence within 90 days | Survey |
| **Old system retirement** | 0% usage by [date] | System analytics |
| **Business outcome** | [Specific KPI] improved by [X%] | Analytics dashboard |

### What to Do When Adoption Stalls

1. **Diagnose:** Which ADKAR element is the gap?
2. **Segment:** Who's adopting and who isn't? What's different about the groups?
3. **Intervene:** Targeted action for the specific gap (more communication, more training, removing barriers)
4. **Escalate if needed:** If barriers are systemic (tools, processes, leadership support), escalate
5. **Adjust timeline:** Better to extend than to force adoption that won't stick
