# Facilitation

> Workshop design, meeting facilitation techniques, and group dynamics management. Applicable across research, design, strategy, and project management.

---

## Workshop Design

### Planning Template

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Objective** | What decision, alignment, or output should this workshop produce? |
| **Participants** | Who needs to be there? (5-8 is ideal, 12 max) |
| **Duration** | How long? (60 min for focused topics, half-day for complex) |
| **Pre-work** | What should participants read/prepare before? |
| **Agenda** | Timed activity sequence (see below) |
| **Materials** | Physical: sticky notes, markers, whiteboard. Digital: Miro, FigJam, shared doc |
| **Output** | What artifact leaves the room? (decisions, prioritized list, action items) |

### Agenda Design Principles

- **Energy arc:** Start with warm-up, build to the hardest work in the middle, wind down with synthesis
- **Mix activities:** Alternate between individual thinking, small group, and full group
- **Time-box everything:** Parkinson's law applies — work expands to fill the time
- **Build in breaks:** 5 min every 45-60 min, 15 min every 90 min
- **End with clarity:** Last 15 minutes are always synthesis + next steps

### Generic Workshop Structure

| Time | Activity | Mode | Purpose |
|------|----------|------|---------|
| 0:00 | Welcome + objectives | Full group | Align on goals |
| 0:10 | Context setting | Presenter | Share relevant information |
| 0:20 | Individual reflection | Solo | Everyone forms their own view |
| 0:30 | Small group discussion | Pairs/trios | Develop and pressure-test ideas |
| 0:50 | Share-out | Full group | Surface key themes |
| 1:05 | Prioritization / voting | Full group | Converge on direction |
| 1:20 | Synthesis + next steps | Full group | Capture decisions, assign actions |
| 1:30 | Close | Full group | Thank participants, confirm follow-up |

## Facilitation Techniques

### Timeboxing

**What:** Set strict time limits for each activity and enforce them.

**Why:** Prevents discussions from spiraling. Creates productive urgency.

**How:**
- Use a visible timer (phone, projected, Miro timer)
- Announce time remaining at halfway and 1 minute before end
- When time is up, stop. Move parking lot items to follow-up.
- Build in 5 min buffer per hour for overruns

### Parking Lot

**What:** A visible space to capture off-topic but valid points for later discussion.

**Why:** Validates the contributor without derailing the current topic.

**How:**
- Physical: Corner of whiteboard labeled "Parking Lot"
- Digital: Dedicated sticky note area or running list
- Say: "Great point — I'll add that to our parking lot so we don't lose it"
- Review parking lot at end of session, assign follow-ups

### Round-Robin

**What:** Go around the room, each person speaks in turn.

**Why:** Ensures everyone contributes. Prevents dominant voices from controlling.

**When to use:**
- Opening check-ins
- Sharing critique or feedback
- When you notice some people haven't spoken

**Variation — Popcorn:** After each person speaks, they pick who goes next. More natural but can leave quiet people for last.

### Silent Writing (Brainwriting)

**What:** Everyone writes ideas independently before any discussion.

**Why:** Prevents anchoring on the first idea shared. Equal participation regardless of personality.

**How:**
1. Pose the question or prompt clearly
2. Set timer (3-5 minutes)
3. Everyone writes silently (sticky notes, digital cards)
4. Share and group after writing is complete
5. Discuss themes, not individual contributions

### Dot Voting

**What:** Each participant gets a fixed number of votes (stickers or marks) to allocate.

**Why:** Quick prioritization that gives everyone equal voice.

**How:**
- Display all options visually
- Each person gets 3-5 dots
- Vote silently (no discussion during voting)
- Can place multiple dots on one item if feeling strongly
- Count and discuss the results

**Variations:**
- Priority dots: Different colors for different criteria (impact, feasibility)
- Forced ranking: Must spread dots across at least 3 items

### Think-Pair-Share

**What:** Individual thinking → discuss with a partner → share with the group.

**Why:** Builds confidence before sharing with a large group. Develops ideas through dialogue.

**How:**
1. Pose the question (1-2 minutes individual thought)
2. Pair up and discuss (3-5 minutes)
3. Each pair shares their key takeaway with the group

## Group Dynamics Management

### Dominant Voices

**Signs:** One person talks more than everyone else combined. Others defer or shut down.

**Tactics:**
- Use round-robin to distribute speaking time
- Start with silent writing before discussion
- Directly invite quieter participants: "[Name], what's your perspective?"
- Time-box individual contributions: "30 seconds per person"
- After the dominant speaker finishes, ask: "Who has a different perspective?"

### Quiet Participants

**Signs:** Some people haven't spoken. They may be processing, intimidated, or disengaged.

**Tactics:**
- Use small groups (pairs/trios) before full group — safer to share
- Silent activities (writing, voting) don't require speaking
- Ask directly but gently: "[Name], I'd value your perspective on this"
- Avoid put-on-the-spot questions — give them a moment to prepare: "I'll come to you next"
- Check in during breaks: "Is there anything you'd like to raise?"

### Tangents

**Signs:** Discussion drifts from the topic. Energy goes somewhere unproductive.

**Tactics:**
- Park it: "Interesting — let me add that to the parking lot and we'll come back to it"
- Redirect: "How does this connect to [the topic we're focused on]?"
- Time-check: "We have 10 minutes left for this section — let's stay focused on [topic]"
- Validate then redirect: "That's an important point. For today, let's focus on [scope]"

### Conflict

**Signs:** Disagreement escalates. People talk over each other. Body language shifts.

**Tactics:**
- Name it: "I'm sensing some tension here. Let's make sure we hear both perspectives"
- Separate the people from the idea: "Let's evaluate the idea on its merits"
- Ask each side to articulate the other's position: "Can you restate what [Name] is saying?"
- Find common ground: "You both want [shared goal] — you disagree about [specific point]"
- If unresolvable: "Let's capture both positions and take this offline with [decision-maker]"

### Low Energy

**Signs:** People check phones. Responses are short. Engagement drops.

**Tactics:**
- Take a break (standing, walking, snacks)
- Switch activity mode (move from discussion to sketching, or from full group to pairs)
- Change the physical setup (stand up, move to a different space)
- Ask a provocative question to re-engage: "What's the most controversial thing about this?"
- Acknowledge it: "Energy seems low — let's take 5 and come back fresh"

## Remote Facilitation

### Tools

| Tool Type | Options | Used For |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| Video | Zoom, Meet, Teams | Face-to-face discussion |
| Whiteboard | Miro, FigJam, Mural | Visual collaboration, sticky notes, voting |
| Documents | Google Docs, Notion | Real-time collaborative writing |
| Polling | Slido, Mentimeter | Quick votes, pulse checks |
| Timer | Miro timer, cuckoo.team | Visible timeboxing |

### Remote-Specific Challenges and Fixes

| Challenge | Fix |
|-----------|-----|
| Cameras off = disengagement | Request cameras on. Model it yourself. |
| Talking over each other | Use hand-raise feature or chat "+" to signal |
| Hard to read the room | Poll more frequently. Use emoji reactions. |
| Tech issues eat time | Send tool links and instructions 24h before |
| Multitasking | Keep activities short (5-10 min each), require active input |

### Breakout Rooms

**When to use:** Groups of 6+, when small group discussion is needed.

**Setup:**
- 2-4 people per room
- Clear prompt displayed (on screen or in chat)
- Specific deliverable: "Come back with your top 2 ideas"
- Set timer: 5-10 minutes
- Visit rooms briefly to check progress

### Async Pre-Work

**Why:** Remote workshops are more tiring. Front-load context so workshop time is for discussion, not presentation.

**What to send:**
- Context document (maximum 2 pages)
- Specific prompt: "Come prepared with your top 3 ideas for [topic]"
- Any data or research needed for informed discussion
- Send 48-72 hours before (not the night before)

## Common Workshop Formats

### Design Sprint (5-Day or Compressed)

| Day | Focus | Key Activity |
|-----|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Understand | Map the challenge, expert interviews, HMW |
| 2 | Sketch | Individual solution sketching, Crazy 8s |
| 3 | Decide | Review, vote, pick direction, storyboard |
| 4 | Prototype | Build a realistic prototype |
| 5 | Test | Test with 5 real users, synthesize findings |

**Compressed version (2-day):** Combine Day 1-2 and Day 3-4.

### Strategy Offsite

**Duration:** Half-day to full day
**Participants:** Leadership team

| Block | Duration | Activity |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Context setting | 30 min | Review market data, performance, customer feedback |
| Vision alignment | 45 min | What does success look like in 12 months? |
| Strategic options | 60 min | Identify and evaluate strategic choices |
| Prioritization | 45 min | Rank options, allocate resources |
| Action planning | 30 min | Define next steps, owners, timelines |

### Brainstorming Session

**Duration:** 60-90 minutes

| Phase | Duration | Activity |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Frame | 10 min | Define the challenge, share constraints |
| Diverge | 30-40 min | Generate ideas (silent writing → share → build) |
| Converge | 15-20 min | Dot vote, discuss top ideas |
| Plan | 10 min | Assign next steps for top ideas |

### Alignment Workshop

**Duration:** 90-120 minutes
**Use when:** Teams are misaligned on direction, priorities, or approach

| Phase | Duration | Activity |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Current state | 20 min | Each team shares their perspective |
| Gap identification | 20 min | Where do views differ? (make conflicts explicit) |
| Root cause | 20 min | Why do we see things differently? (data, priorities, incentives) |
| Alignment | 30 min | What can we agree on? What needs escalation? |
| Commitments | 15 min | Specific agreements and follow-up actions |

## Post-Workshop Follow-Through

### Synthesis (Within 24 Hours)

- Photograph or export all workshop artifacts
- Compile key decisions, themes, and outputs into a summary document
- Highlight areas of agreement and areas of disagreement
- Note parking lot items and who will address them

### Action Items

| Action | Owner | Deadline | Status |
|--------|-------|----------|--------|
| [Specific action from workshop] | [Name] | [Date] | [Open/Done] |

### Communication

- Send workshop summary to all participants within 24 hours
- Share relevant outputs with stakeholders who weren't in the room
- Schedule any follow-up meetings identified during the session
- Track action items in your project management tool

### Common Follow-Through Failures

- **No summary sent:** People forget what was decided. Energy dissipates.
- **No action owners:** Good ideas die because nobody is accountable.
- **No follow-up check:** Actions assigned but never tracked or completed.
- **Treating the workshop as the end:** The workshop is the beginning. The work starts after.
