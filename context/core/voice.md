## Language Rules

(Specific rules about how you write. Be as detailed as possible. Example:)
(- Active voice always. "We built this" not "This was built by us.")
(- Specific over vague. "Saves 10 hours/week" not "improves efficiency.")
(- Numbers over adjectives. "3x faster" not "much faster.")
(- No filler words: "basically," "actually," "just," "really," "very.")
(- No hedging: "I think maybe" → "Here's what I'd do.")
(- Contractions are fine — "don't" not "do not.")
(- Oxford comma: yes/no.)

## Words to Avoid

(List specific words and phrases that don't belong in your communication. Be exhaustive — the more you list, the cleaner the output. Example:)
(- "Leverage" — say "use")
(- "Best-in-class" — say what specifically makes it good)
(- "Solutions" without specifics — say what the solution actually is)
(- "Innovative" — show the innovation instead)
(- "Synergy" — describe the actual benefit)
(- "Cutting-edge" — say what's new about it)
(- "Unlock potential" — say what specifically happens)
(- No emojis unless specifically requested)
(- No em dashes — use periods or commas)
(- No exclamation marks in professional content)
