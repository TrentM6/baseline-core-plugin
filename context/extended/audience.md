## Primary Audience

(- They care about: developer productivity, team health, shipping velocity)

## Buyer vs. User

(If the person who buys is different from the person who uses, describe both. Example:)
(- Buyer: VP Engineering — cares about ROI, team retention, delivery metrics)
(- User: Team lead — cares about daily workflow, reducing friction, visibility)
(- Champion: Senior IC who discovers the tool and advocates internally)

## Pain Points

(What problems does your audience have that you solve? Be specific. Example:)
(- Spending 40% of time in status meetings instead of building)
(- No visibility into what's actually blocking the team)
(- Can't quantify engineering impact to leadership)
(- Team morale is dropping but they can't pinpoint why)

## Buying Triggers

(What makes someone look for a solution like yours right now? These are the moments that create urgency. Example:)
(- Just had a bad quarter — shipped late, team is frustrated)
(- New VP joined and wants visibility into what the team is doing)
(- Team grew from 10 to 30 and old processes broke)
(- Board is asking for engineering metrics they can't produce)
(- Lost a key engineer and realized they have no documentation)

## What They've Tried

(What alternatives or workarounds has your audience already attempted? Example:)
(- Spreadsheets and manual tracking — doesn't scale)
(- Enterprise platforms — too complex, too expensive, too slow to deploy)
(- Built internal dashboards — maintenance burden, nobody trusts the data)
(- Hired a consultant — expensive, recommendations didn't stick)

## Decision Process

(How does your audience evaluate and buy? Example:)
(- Discovery: finds you through content, referral, or search)
(- Evaluation: tries free tier or requests demo)
(- Champion builds internal case: ROI calculation, comparison to alternatives)
(- Decision maker approves: usually VP or founder)
(- Timeline: 1-2 weeks for small teams, 1-3 months for larger orgs)
(- Procurement: self-serve for small teams, procurement process for enterprise)
